Phoebe Lam

Pre-Business

My name is Phoebe Lam and I am currently a Pre-Business undergraduate student at the University of California-Riverside, with a 3.66 GPA as of the end of the Fall 2019 quarter. I plan to concentrate in either Marketing or Finance, looking to explore in both fields or maybe integrate the two in my future career. Experience in both would propel me closer to my current ambitions of becoming an entrepreneur. While I do not have paid work experience, I have a lot of volunteer experience that have helped me to gain both soft and hard skills essential in completing any task at hand.

I am extremely adaptable and organized, which came in handy during my time in the Placentia Teen Advisory Council. All events and fundraisers were catered to the Placentia population of all ages, so it was important to choose the proper location, date, and time best to maximize turnout and profit. For example, we held a fundraiser for the Placentia public library in the park the day a concert was scheduled. Originally, we were going to have the fundraising event in front of the library on a random Saturday because the library garnered the most patrons on Saturday. However, I suggested to switch it around because while there may have been a lot of people, not enough would purchase the food/drink items at our booth. But at a concert, the influx of hungry and thirsty concert-goers was much higher. But my proposition proved to be more challenging because it required more time and effort to select a location that wasn't our primary. The library was the Council's go-to due to convenience and significantly more available employees and volunteers. Therefore, some didn't agree with me. But I was able to encourage my peers to carry out our mission with the same rigor as before and our event turned out to be a profitable success.

In addition, I have strong communication skills, which I can attribute to both my time in the Advisory Council and at the St. Jude Medical Center. At St. Jude's, I worked a 3-hour shift every Friday (one of the busier days), handling both visitors and patients. I checked visitors in and guided them to the right rooms, if needed. I also retrieved wheelchairs and discharged patients from any part of the hospital. Being around so many different people really opened my mind to the different thoughts, beliefs, outlooks, and behaviors each person has. The constant interaction with St. Jude patrons and cooperation with fellow volunteers gave me an idea of what kind of work life I wanted. The ideal work place for me would promote teamwork and frequent communication with others. I thrive best in proactive environments.

However, I have also worked in quieter environments, such as when I was an intern at the University of California-Irvine. The majority of the internship was preparing to assist carrying out a lab on electro-acupuncture. I attended lectures and eventually was able to have a small part in the research lab. And while this internship had nothing to do with my choice discipline, I found that I also enjoyed studious circumstances; this was not necessarily due to the serenity of the internship, but largely because I was able to learn a lot from it. While I am not a big fan of science, I am a big fan of acquiring knowledge. Time is limited and I'd like it best if I could use my time to absorb all that I can because all knowledge is useful knowledge at one point or another.

Being a student, I can only say that I want to best utilize that time and find ways to not only improve myself, but to be of service to the community as well. One thing in common among my past volunteer experiences is that, no matter how different they may seem, they were all geared towards community. My goal is to provide my best efforts to benefit as many people as possible, especially since I am able to consider all points of view and can work well with anyone, even those whom I may not concur with. I have the expertise imperative in undertaking any endeavor and hope to build that expertise.
